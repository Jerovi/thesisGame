Exploring Our Planets

The solar system consists of eight planets orbiting the Sun. Each planet has unique characteristics, from rocky inner planets like Earth to gas giants like Jupiter.

Planets are divided into terrestrial (Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars) and jovian (Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune). Earth is the only known planet with life.

Did you know? Jupiter is the largest planet, with a diameter over 11 times that of Earth, and Saturn is famous for its stunning rings made of ice and rock.

Planet Facts

  • Mercury: Closest to the Sun, no atmosphere.
  • Venus: Hottest planet, thick toxic atmosphere.
  • Earth: Only planet with liquid water and life.
  • Mars: Known as the Red Planet, potential for past life.
  • Jupiter: Largest planet, has over 90 moons.
  • Saturn: Famous for its rings, made of ice.
  • Uranus: Rotates on its side, icy blue color.
  • Neptune: Farthest planet, strong winds up to 1,200 mph.
